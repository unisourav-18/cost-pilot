# CostPilot — User Interviews

## Methodology

Conducted lightweight outreach via LinkedIn DM to gather early signal on the problem hypothesis before finalizing the product. The goal was not to validate the solution but to stress-test whether the underlying problem — untracked AI subscription spend — is felt as a real pain point by people in the target market.

**Outreach message summarized:** Described the tool as an AI Subscription Audit Engine that helps individuals and startups audit active AI subscriptions, assess ROI, and discover smarter alternatives. Asked for honest feedback on whether this solves a real problem and what they would add or do differently.

---

## Interview 1

**Name:** Akhil Chaudhary   
**Method:** LinkedIn DM  
**Date:** This week  

### Key Quotes

> "I think this solves a real problem, especially now when many startups and individuals are subscribing to multiple AI tools without properly tracking actual usage or ROI."

> "The profitability/ROI assessment angle makes it more actionable instead of just being a subscription tracker."

> "Has the website or MVP been completed yet? Would love to check it out if you already have a demo or landing page ready."

### Feedback Summary

Akhil validated the core problem immediately and without prompting — untracked AI subscriptions are a felt pain point, particularly for startups trying to reduce unnecessary SaaS costs. He highlighted three specific feature ideas:

- **Usage-based recommendations** — suggesting one tool that replaces 2–3 subscriptions
- **Monthly savings dashboard** — a visual showing savings potential over time
- **Team-level analytics and duplicate subscription detection** — particularly relevant for startup teams where multiple people independently subscribe to overlapping tools

He also asked to see the MVP, which signals genuine interest beyond polite validation.

### What This Confirmed

- The problem is real and recognized without needing explanation
- The ROI/savings framing is more compelling than a simple tracker framing
- Duplicate detection (already built) resonates with the target user
- There is pull toward a demo — the shareable audit URL addresses this directly

### What This Challenged

- The current product shows spend and savings at a point in time but has no persistent dashboard showing savings over time — this is a week 2 feature
- Usage-based recommendations require actual usage data, which the current form doesn't collect — worth adding as an optional field

## Recruitment Process

Outreach was sent to professionals in the tech and startup ecosystem via LinkedIn. The message was personalized, transparent about being in early research phase, and asked for honest critique rather than validation. Recipients were chosen based on relevance to the target user profile — people working at or with early-stage startups who are likely to have direct exposure to AI tool adoption decisions.

## What Would Improve This Section

One interview is a starting point, not a conclusion. The next step is 4–9 more conversations across different user types:

| User Type | What to Learn |
|---|---|
| Founding engineer at a 5–15 person startup | Do they feel the pain personally or does it fall to ops? |
| Technical co-founder / CTO | Who owns the AI budget decision? |
| Ops / finance lead at a Series A startup | How do they currently track SaaS spend? Is AI a line item? |
| Solo developer / indie hacker | Is the pain acute enough to act on, or just mildly annoying? |

Target: 5 interviews minimum before making any significant product changes based on user feedback.
