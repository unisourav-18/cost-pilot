# ECONOMICS — CostPilot (Credex)

## 1. Value of a Converted Lead (to Credex)

A “converted lead” here = a startup that:
1) runs an audit  
2) books a Credex consultation  
3) eventually buys credits / uses Credex infrastructure or services  

Assumption:
- CostPilot attracts **AI-heavy startups spending $300–$1500/month on tools**
- These are exactly the same companies likely to adopt Credex for optimization, infra, or credit-based cost management

### Revenue pathways for Credex:
- Consulting / optimization upsell: $500–$2,000
- Credit platform adoption: $100–$500/month recurring equivalent margin
- Enterprise conversion later: $5K–$20K annualized potential

### Conservative blended estimate:

- 1 converted lead lifetime value (LTV) ≈ **$1,200**

Reasoning:
- 30% take a small paid engagement (~$500)
- 10% convert into recurring infra usage (~$200–$500 ARR equivalent)
- weighted average across early-stage startups

## 3. Funnel Conversion Model

Let’s define funnel:

### Step 1: Audit Completed → Consultation Booked
- Strong pain signal users only
- Estimated conversion: **8% – 15%**

We assume:
👉 **12% conversion**

---

### Step 2: Consultation → Credex Purchase
- Depends on urgency + cost savings clarity
- Estimated conversion: **20% – 30%**

We assume:
👉 **25% conversion**

---

### Final Funnel Conversion:

Audit user → paying Credex customer  
= 12% × 25%  
= **3% overall conversion rate**

## 6. Key Conditions for Success

For this model to work, these must be true:

### 1. Strong pain validation
Startups must *feel AI spend waste immediately*

### 2. Audit must feel “insightful enough”
Not generic → must show real savings opportunities

### 3. High shareability
Users must want to:
- screenshot results
- post “AI stack savings”
- compare with other founders

### 4. Credex upsell must be natural
Not forced → should feel like:
> “this saves money if we optimize infra / credits / usage”

## Bottom Line

CostPilot is not a SaaS tool — it is a **high-intent lead generator disguised as a product**.

Economically:
- Low CAC distribution channels
- High-intent founder audience
- Strong conversion when pain is visible
- Clear upsell path into Credex ecosystem

If distribution works, unit economics are strongly favorable even with conservative assumptions.
